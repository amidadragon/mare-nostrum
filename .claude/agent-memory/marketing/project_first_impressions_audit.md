First impressions audit conducted March 2026. Based on CLAUDE.md + RETENTION_BRAINSTORM.md read-through.

**Critical gaps found:**

1. No designed "GET IT" moment in first 10 minutes. The companions (crow, cat, tortoise) are the best candidate — one personality beat from the crow in first 10 minutes would do it.

2. No unresolved thread at session end. Players have nothing pulling them back tomorrow without the Hanno rotating merchant + NPC daily wants (not yet built).

3. The procedural audio USP is undemonstrable on the itch.io page. Needs either embedded audio, animated GIF showing lyre respond to context, or extremely specific copy.

4. 26-building menu with no guidance at home island arrival = first quit point (min 2-5).

5. No "overview camera" moment where players see their whole colony at once — the shareable payoff image doesn't exist as a designed moment.

**Highest-leverage single change:** A 60-second uncut screen recording of the first 60 seconds of play on the itch.io page. No text, no HUD callouts. Just atmosphere. This is what drives wishlists in cozy games.

**Most shareable moment that doesn't exist yet:** Hyperborea Ice Mirror showing your castaway form — pure narrative, zero gameplay cost, extremely clip-worthy. Necropolis Room 13 (ghost, one line, no reward) is second.

**Quit points:**
- Minute 2-5: home island, no clear first action
- Minute 15-20: resource chain confusion, unclear failure feedback
- Hour 1: NPC quest stall, world goes static
- Post-Chapter III-IV: hearts maxed, NPCs become scenery
- Invisible long-term: no daily hook, players close tab and don't return

**Steam trailer key insight:** The trailer lives or dies on the audio. The procedural lyre shifting mode as environment changes must be the last beat before title card — demonstrated, not explained.

**YouTuber angle:** "How a browser game generates music with no audio files" — devlog-style, technical credibility, does not require game to be famous first.

**Why:** Written to ground future copy and feature prioritization discussions.
**How to apply:** Reference when writing store copy, trailer briefs, or prioritizing dev work with marketing impact.
