### Shipwrecked. Sunlit. Reborn.

You wake on a wreck-strewn beach with salt in your lungs and nothing to your name. Planks from a broken trireme. A sun already burning gold off the water. Somewhere past the horizon, there are islands, and people, and a life worth rebuilding.

Mare Nostrum is the game you play when you want to be somewhere warm and build something that matters.

---

### Zero External Assets. Every Pixel. Every Note. Procedural.

This is the part that matters: **there are no imported assets in this game. None.**

No sprite sheets downloaded from a marketplace. No audio files. No tileset packs. No fonts ripped from a library. Every single pixel on screen is drawn in code. Every note you hear — the lyre while you farm, the combat drums, the eerie tones in the underworld, the waves, the wind, the crickets — is synthesized live, in real time, from oscillators.

The lyre plays in Dorian mode while you tend crops. When enemies appear, it shifts to Phrygian. In the Necropolis, it drops to sparse, reverbed tones that sound like stone and old air. The music doesn't just play — it *listens* to the game and responds at the note level.

36,000 lines of JavaScript. 19 source files. Zero dependencies beyond p5.js. The entire game lives in a single browser tab.

**This is what a game looks like when one person builds every atom of it by hand.**

### Features

- **10-chapter narrative** — from shipwrecked exile to Imperator of a multi-island Roman colony
- **4 explorable islands** — a living volcano, frozen ruins, spice trade routes, and the underworld
- **Colony building** — 26 building types, from a lean-to on day one to a stone granary, smithy, and colony hall
- **The Imperial Bridge** — build a stone causeway with Roman pillars across open water to connect your islands
- **Farming & cooking** — plant emmer wheat at dawn, harvest by afternoon, grind to flour, bake bread, gift it to a friend
- **Fishing** — 3-phase bobber system, multiple fish species, a growing codex
- **Diving** — underwater exploration with bioluminescent depths, sea treasure, and a breath bar
- **Combat** — 9-skill tree, 9 enemy types, arena waves, boss encounters in volcanic craters
- **4 NPCs with full quest chains** — a senator's exiled daughter, a veteran hunting his lost legion's eagle, a crystal prophet, and one who's harder to read
- **4 AI companions** — two cats, a tortoise, and a crow with opinions about your inventory
- **Procedural soundtrack** — lyre, ambient layers, combat percussion, all synthesized live with zero audio files
- **Full day/night cycle** — seasons, weather, solar energy mechanics
- **Island progression** — Citizen > Governor > Senator > Consul > Imperator (25 levels)
- **Character transformation** — your sprite evolves from castaway rags to Imperator's laurel as your colony grows
- **Browser-native** — no download, no install, no account. Open the tab. Play.

### What Makes It Unique

Most indie games use engines, asset packs, audio libraries. Mare Nostrum uses none of them.

The entire visual world — every tree, every building, every NPC, every wave on the water, every particle of pollen drifting through golden-hour light — is drawn procedurally in p5.js. The entire audio world — every lyre note, every ambient cricket, every combat hit — is synthesized from oscillators. There is not a single imported image or sound file in the project.

This isn't a limitation. It's the point. The warm Mediterranean palette, the bioluminescent diving zones, the way sunset light hits stone differently than wood — these exist because every pixel was a deliberate choice, not a tile dropped from a sheet.

If Stardew Valley sailed the Mediterranean, was built from literal scratch, and hired a Roman historian as narrative director — this is that game.

### The World

**Home Island** — Where you wash ashore. Farm plots, four NPCs, your first buildings. The colony that becomes home.

**Terra Nova** — The wild island you colonize mid-game. A second settlement, trade routes, and the far end of the Imperial Bridge.

**Isle of Vulcan** — Lava pools, smoke vents, a forge altar in the crater. Where you make the Blade of Vulcan from iron and titan bone.

**Hyperborea** — Frozen ruins. Lost Roman soldiers. The truth about Legion IX Hispana — the real legion that vanished from history.

**Isle of Plenty** — Spice routes, trade, warmth. The economic engine of your growing empire.

**The Necropolis** — The underworld. The final inscription. The real reason you were exiled. You earn this one.

### The Story

Ten chapters. A full arc.

- **Chapter I** — Scavenge the wreck. Repair your trireme. Sail to safety.
- **Chapter III** — Livia's secret. A senator's daughter, exiled for blood.
- **Chapter IV** — Marcus and the lost eagle of the Ninth Legion.
- **Chapter VI** — Build the Imperial Bridge across open water, stone by stone.
- **Chapter VII** — The Volcanic Forge. Iron + titan bone. Something that will last.
- **Chapter IX** — Enter the Necropolis. Read the final inscription.
- **Chapter X** — The Rite of Mare Nostrum. Not by conquest. By love.

The narrative draws on real Roman history — the disappearance of Legion IX Hispana, exile as political weapon in the Republic, and the phrase *mare nostrum* (our sea) as both imperial claim and something closer to home.

### Technical Details

**No download. No install. No account.**

- Runs in any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
- Mobile-playable with touch controls (iOS and Android)
- Saves to localStorage — close the tab, come back days later, pick up where you left off
- Installable as a PWA for offline play
- 60fps on any machine from the last 8 years
- Controls: WASD/arrows, click/tap to move, E to interact, left-click to attack, Esc for inventory
