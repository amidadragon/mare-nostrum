## LONG DESCRIPTION

---

**Shipwrecked. Sunlit. Reborn.**

You wake on a wreck-strewn beach. Salt in your lungs, planks scattered around you, the sun already burning gold off the water. You have nothing. Somewhere beyond the horizon is an island — and people — and a life worth building.

That's the beginning. What happens next is yours.

---

Mare Nostrum is a cozy Roman life-sim / survival-crafting game that plays in your browser, right now, with no download required. You are a Roman exile. You start with wreckage. You end as Imperator of a multi-island colony you built with your own hands.

If Stardew Valley sailed the Mediterranean and hired a Roman historian as narrative director, this is the game they'd make.

---

### Farming & Crafting

Plant emmer wheat in the morning, harvest it by afternoon, grind it to flour, bake it into bread, gift the bread to a friend who needs it. The chain is satisfying — every step visible, every output earned. Crops grow in real time through a full day/night cycle. Seasons shift. Weather rolls in. A solar energy system powers your buildings through the night.

There are 20 building types to construct, from a simple lean-to on your first morning to a stone granary, a fishing dock, a smithy, and eventually a colony hall on the second island you conquer across open water.

---

### Combat & Exploration

The Mediterranean is not entirely peaceful. Combat is direct — left-click to strike — but the real reward is what you find after: sea treasure in the diving zones, lore tablets buried in frozen ruins on Hyperborea, forge materials in a volcano's crater.

There are four islands beyond your home, each with its own atmosphere and its own secrets:

- **Isle of Vulcan** — lava pools, forge altars, the Blade of Vulcan waiting to be made
- **Hyperborea** — frozen ruins, lost Roman soldiers, the truth about Legion IX Hispana
- **Isle of Plenty** — spice routes, trade, warmth
- **The Necropolis** — the underworld. The inscription on the final wall. The real reason you were exiled.

---

### NPCs & Relationships

Four people live on your home island. Each arrived the same way you did — broken, on purpose, by someone with power. Get to know them.

- **Livia** — senator's daughter, exiled for what she knows about her father
- **Marcus** — veteran of the Legions, looking for the lost eagle standard of the IX Hispana
- **Vesta** — a prophet who reads crystal and speaks in conditionals
- **Felix** — harder to read than the others

Each has a three-quest personal chain that unfolds through gifts, time, and trust. The payoffs are worth the patience.

---

### Companions

Four autonomous AI companions wander the island on their own schedules — a pair of cats, a tortoise, a crow. They're not decorative. They have preferences. The crow has opinions about your inventory choices.

---

### Colony Building & The Imperial Bridge

Mid-game, you conquer the wild island of Terra Nova and begin building your second colony. This is where Mare Nostrum opens up: trade routes between islands, a second set of production chains, a growing population to govern.

And then: the Imperial Bridge. A stone causeway with Roman pillars, balustrades, and torches, spanning open water between your two islands. You build it stone by stone. When you walk it for the first time, it feels earned.

Island progression runs 25 levels: Citizen → Governor → Senator → Consul → Imperator.

---

### The Story

Ten chapters. A full arc.

- Chapter I: Scavenge the wreck. Repair your trireme. Sail to safety.
- Chapter III: Livia's secret — and why it matters.
- Chapter IV: Marcus and the lost eagle.
- Chapter VI: Build the Imperial Bridge.
- Chapter VII: The Volcanic Forge. Iron + titan bone.
- Chapter IX: Enter the Necropolis. Read the final inscription.
- Chapter X: The Rite of Mare Nostrum. Not by conquest. By love.

---

### Audio

The music you hear while farming — that lyre — is not a file. Every note is synthesized live, in real time, using oscillators tuned to Dorian mode. When enemies appear, the mode shifts to Phrygian. In the Necropolis, it drops to sparse, slow, reverbed tones that feel like stone and old air.

Ambient sound works the same way: waves, wind, birdsong, crickets, the low rumble of Vulcan's crater — all synthesized. Zero audio files in the project. The soundscape responds to game state at the note level, not just volume.

It sounds like nothing else you've heard in a browser game, because it's made differently.

---

### What Makes It Special

Most browser games compromise. Mare Nostrum does not.

It's built on p5.js — no engine, no install, no storefront layer between you and the game. The entire codebase lives in a single HTML page that opens and runs. Your save lives in your browser's localStorage. You can close the tab and come back three days later, mid-chapter, exactly where you left.

The Roman setting is not aesthetic decoration. The narrative draws on real history — the disappearance of the Ninth Legion, the meaning of exile as a political weapon in the Republic, the phrase "mare nostrum" (our sea) as both imperial claim and something closer to home. The lore is worth reading.

This is a game about rebuilding. About quiet mornings planting grain when everything else has been taken. About what it means to make something that lasts.

---

### System Requirements

**No download. No install. No account.**

- Desktop: any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge) — runs on the open tab
- Mobile: playable on iOS and Android in a mobile browser, touch controls included
- Save: localStorage — your exile persists across sessions automatically
- Performance: runs at 60fps on any machine made in the last 8 years

Controls: WASD or arrow keys, click/tap to move, E to interact, left-click to attack, Esc for inventory and journal.
