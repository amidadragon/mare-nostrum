# ATLANTIS VISION — Mare Nostrum Island Evolution
## From Castaway Camp to Glowing Megalopolis

**The emotional arc in one sentence:** You didn't just survive — you built a myth.

The player never looks at a progress bar. They look at their island and see what they've made. The transformation from a dying campfire on a beach to a crystal-ringed city that glows across the sea IS the story. Every era shift should feel like crossing a threshold the player earned.

---

## HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT

Each era describes the island as a living, breathing place — not a checklist of features. When building any element, ask: does this make the player feel what this era is supposed to feel? If not, cut it or change it.

The three feelings to hit:

- **Era 1:** "I'm just trying to survive. But look — I'm still here."
- **Era 2:** "I'm building something real. Rome would recognize this."
- **Era 3:** "What have I made? This isn't possible. This is mine."

## ERA 1: THE VILLAGE (Levels 1–8)
### "A fire in the dark"

---

### 1. Visual Transformation of the Island Surface

The island at Level 1 is barely claimed. Most of the surface is wild — coarse grass in dull olive-green, patches of exposed brown earth where the player has walked. The coastline reads as hostile: dark wet sand, tide pools catching orange light from the campfire, seabirds picking at nothing useful.

By Level 4, the cleared land expands. Muddy furrows appear near the farming plots. The first wooden fence posts break the wildness into something intentional. Trampled earth paths connect the campfire to the well to the garden — not placed by a town planner, worn in by daily use.

By Level 8, the island feels domesticated but not dominated. The grass is greener in tended patches. A hand-dug irrigation ditch runs from the well. The wooden dock creaks with personality. The color palette stays warm earth: amber, ochre, clay red, forest green. Nothing gleams. Everything looks like it was made by someone who needed it to work.

**Color palette:** Warm earth tones. Amber firelight. Ochre soil. Dull olive grass. Weathered driftwood gray. No pure white, no cold blue — this era breathes warmth and effort.

---

### 2. Building Style Evolution

**Level 1–3:** Lashed driftwood, thatched palm fronds, makeshift. Nothing is level or square. The campfire is the emotional center — a circle of rocks with a fire that the player can actually tend. The first sleeping hut is barely more than a lean-to. The storage crate is literally a crate from the shipwreck.

**Level 4–6:** First real construction. Mud-brick walls, thatch roofs with overhangs, wooden shutters. The farmhouse has a garden bed in front of it. The well has a crank and a rope bucket. Buildings cluster slightly — they face each other like neighbors who've learned to coexist.

**Level 7–8:** Roman instincts are beginning to show. The first stone foundation appears under the granary — not grand, but intentional. The gate posts at the dock get carved with a rough ship relief, the player's private monument to how they arrived. The NPC houses have window boxes with herbs. The dirt paths are now packed and stable.

**Materials:** Driftwood → rough-cut timber → squared timber + mud brick + first rough stone. All surface textures should read as handmade. No two planks are identical.

---

### 3. Infrastructure

**Water:** A hand-dug well with a rope and bucket. Manual labor, one pail at a time. The irrigation ditch is a single carved line in the earth — functional but fragile-looking.

**Roads:** None at first. Just worn-earth desire paths — slightly lighter brown than the surrounding soil, indicating foot traffic. By Level 7, flat river stones are laid down on the main paths. Not paved. Just practical.

**Light:** A single campfire at the center. By Level 5, a second fire at the dock entrance. By Level 8, hanging oil lanterns on the gate posts — ceramic, flame flickering visibly.

**Storage/logistics:** Handcart parked behind the granary. Barrels stacked against the barn wall. Everything reads as one person and their four companions managing by hand.

---

### 4. Ambient Life

**Level 1–2:** Just the player. Wind. Distant birds. The cats sit near the campfire.

**Level 3–4:** Chickens appear. They wander the farmyard, peck at the ground near the crops, roost on the fence at dusk. Their sounds are specific: clucking while foraging, sudden flurry of wings when startled. The companions (cat, if earned) nap in sun patches.

**Level 5–6:** Livia, Marcus, Vesta, Felix are present and moving — not just standing at their spots. They walk between their homes and the well. They stop at the garden. They sit by the fire at night. Small domestic loops.

**Level 7–8:** The four NPCs have visible routines. Marcus chops wood in the morning. Vesta tends the herb garden. Felix is at the dock watching the horizon. Livia sits reading near the temple foundation. The island feels like a living household, not a map full of quest-givers.

---

### 5. Lighting and Atmosphere

The campfire is the anchor. Its light radius determines how much of the world feels safe versus shadowy. In the early hours of Level 1, the ocean beyond the firelight is dark and suggestive — something out there, the player doesn't know what.

By Level 4, a warm golden light pools across the farmyard in the evening. The well catches a glint. The hanging lanterns create islands of soft amber against the blue-black night. The oil flame flickers on wind, casting moving shadows on the mud-brick walls.

Day: Soft morning haze burns off by midday. The sun hits the thatched roofs and turns them gold. Long afternoon shadows from the trees cross the dirt paths.

The atmosphere says: impermanence made warm. This could be taken away. But right now, it is beautiful.

---

### 6. Sound Evolution

**Era 1 soundscape:**
- Ambient: Waves dominant at all times. Wind in palm fronds. Distant gulls.
- At night: Crickets layer in. The fire crackles with a specific warmth.
- Chickens: quiet clucks, occasional indignant burst.
- Lyre mode: peaceful, sparse, single-string picking. Sounds lonely in a way that is not unpleasant.
- Work sounds: the wet slap of a spade in earth, the creak of the well rope, wood chopping echoing off nothing.
- Rain (when it comes): irregular drops on thatch, then sheets, then the smell implied by silence after.

No music in the middle of the night. Just the fire and the ocean. Those two sounds carry the entire emotional weight of Era 1.

---

### 7. The "Wow Moment"

**Level 5 — First evening with all four NPCs home.**

The player finishes a work day and the sun drops below the horizon. The campfire auto-lights. Marcus comes out of his house carrying a small oil lamp. Livia closes a shuttered window. Felix leans on the dock railing watching the dark water. Vesta puts herbs out to dry on the fence.

The island is tiny. The fire is small. The ocean is enormous around them.

And the player realizes: I built this. These four people have a home because I made one first.

That is the wow moment of Era 1. Not a spectacle. A belonging.

## ERA 2: THE CITY (Levels 9–17)
### "Roma resurgens — Rome rising again"

---

### 1. Visual Transformation of the Island Surface

At Level 9, the island looks like it made a decision. The organic scatter of Era 1 is being organized. The farmland is still wild-edged but the central area has been cleared and leveled. The first paving stones appear as a proper road grid begins. The hill at center (H1) is being graded — you can see where the earth was cut back to make a terrace.

By Level 12, the island has districts. The northeast side is civic — you can see the forum's foundation imposing itself on the landscape. The west is residential, with tightly clustered insulae. The south slope toward the dock is commercial — market stalls, storage, the smell implied by dye vats and smoked fish.

By Level 17, the island's natural contours have been colonized by Roman geometry. The irregular coastline still shows through — this is an island, not a city grid — but the central zone reads unmistakably as a Roman civitas. The hills are assets now, not obstacles. The aqueduct's arches stride across the lower land, carrying water from H2 to the bathhouse. The lighthouse on the east headland is lit.

**Color palette shift:** Warm stone enters. Travertine white, terracotta orange-red, deep brick red for roof tiles. The greens get brighter — cultivated garden green, not wild olive. Gold accents on the temple pediment. The earth tones don't disappear; they just get framed by stone.

---

### 2. Building Style Evolution

**Level 9–11:** The transition period. Wooden structures begin to be replaced or encased in stone. The farmhouse gets a stone base and new tile roof. The granary doubles in size. The temple gets its first proper foundation stone — the original shrine is enclosed within the new structure like a heart inside a chest.

**Level 12–14:** Full Roman vocabulary emerges. Colonnaded porticos. Arched doorways. Red tile roofs on all civic buildings. The insulae are 2-storey — ground floor shops, upper floor apartments, laundry lines between buildings. The forum is open-air with a raised central platform (the Rostra). Carved relief panels begin to appear on public buildings — ships, wheat, olive branches.

**Level 15–17:** Grandeur starts. The temple now has a full flight of steps, Corinthian columns, a triangular pediment with relief carving. The forum has a fountain. The aqueduct is fully functional — arches span across the lower district, water visible running along the top. The castrum (military quarter) occupies a dedicated zone: barracks in neat rows, training ground, armory.

**Materials:** Rough stone → dressed limestone → marble-faced public buildings. Wooden scaffolding visible on buildings still under construction — the city is alive, mid-build.

---

### 3. Infrastructure

**Water:** The aqueduct is the centerpiece infrastructure of Era 2. It should be one of the most impressive structural elements on the island — five arches minimum, the water channel at the top catching light. Where the aqueduct terminates, the bathhouse complex sprawls. Gravity-fed street fountains appear in the residential district — a simple stone lion's head pouring water into a basin.

**Roads:** Proper Via — paved with flat stone slabs, slightly cambered for drainage, with raised stone curbs and stepping stones at intersections. The main road runs from the dock gate to the forum in a straight line. Side streets are cobblestone, narrower. At Level 15, the road signs appear: stone pillars with carved text indicating direction and distance to the dock, temple, market.

**Light:** Iron lamp posts on the main Via, burning oil through the night. The forum has a large central flame-basket on a tall pole — the eternal civic fire, echo of the campfire from Era 1. The lighthouse at the east headland rotates a beam visible across the water.

**Walls:** At Level 13, the castrum wall appears — stone fortifications with a gatehouse on the road approaching from the beach. Not enclosing the whole island, but marking the military zone as Rome-serious.

---

### 4. Ambient Life

The island is populated now — visibly, audibly.

Citizens walk the streets in small groups. A toga-clad merchant haggles at a market stall. A soldier in lorica segmentata walks the castrum perimeter. Two women carry clay water jugs from the fountain. A child chases a chicken. A cart horse stands hitched outside the forum.

These are ambient NPCs — not interactable, but present. Their presence tells the player: this is a city. It functions without me watching it. I built the conditions for these lives.

The four original NPCs (Livia, Marcus, Vesta, Felix) now have expanded roles reflected in their appearance and location — they're no longer just "the four survivors," they're civic figures. Livia is near the library. Marcus oversees the castrum. Vesta runs the market. Felix is at the harbor.

Chickens and cats remain — they're Era 1 survivors now woven into the city's texture, more charming for it.

---

### 5. Lighting and Atmosphere

The lamp posts on the Via cast regular pools of yellow-orange light with dark intervals between — the city at night has a rhythm. The forum's eternal fire is visible from most of the island. The lighthouse beam sweeps the water.

Dawn in Era 2 is different from Era 1. The light hits stone, not thatch. It comes in off the water and catches the temple columns first — they glow orange before the rest of the island is awake. The columns cast long parallel shadows west across the forum stones.

At midday, the market district is loud and hot. Heat shimmer off the stone pavement. The awnings on the market stalls cast striped shadow. It feels alive and full.

At night in rain, the lamp reflections slick across wet cobblestones. The aqueduct gurgles louder in the quiet. The castrum is the only fully lit zone — soldiers don't sleep the same hours as civilians.

The atmosphere says: order. Roman order. Beautiful, oppressive, impressive order that you imposed on a wild island through sheer will.

---

### 6. Sound Evolution

**Era 2 soundscape:**
- The waves are still there but secondary now — the city sounds dominate in the center
- Market: vendor calls, the clink of coins, the muffled negotiation of barter
- Construction: distant hammering, stone scraping on stone, scaffolding creaking
- Civic: the murmur of forum conversation, a crowd response to an address
- Military: rhythmic sword-on-shield drilling from the castrum, commands called out
- The aqueduct: the constant low sound of running water — subtle but always present, like civic pride made audible
- Lyre mode shifts to an organized Roman mode — not a single lyre now but an implied ensemble. Modal harmonics, measured tempo. Feels like civilization.
- At festivals: drums, crowd noise, the procession sound of something being celebrated

The campfire crackle is still there at night in the residential area. It's now nostalgic.

---

### 7. The "Wow Moment"

**Level 12 — First time the player walks the full Via from dock to temple.**

The paved road runs straight. Lamp posts flank it at regular intervals, unlit in daylight but imposing in their regularity. Citizens cross ahead of the player. The market noise bleeds in from the left. Ahead, the temple steps rise at the road's end, columns framing the sky.

The player stops at the top of the temple steps and turns around.

They can see the whole island from here. The aqueduct arches. The forum below. The dock in the distance, still showing the rough wood of Era 1 against the newer stone construction closer in. The lighthouse on the headland. Their four NPCs moving through the streets.

The island they're looking at is a city. Their city.

That is the wow moment of Era 2. Not just pride — recognition. The exile built Rome again, on an island the sea tried to swallow them on.

---

## ERA 3: THE MEGALOPOLIS / ATLANTIS (Levels 18–25)
### "Something impossible. Something ours."

---

### CRITICAL DESIGN NOTE

Era 3 is not a bigger Era 2. It is a transformation into something that doesn't exist in history — that couldn't. The solarpunk-Roman crystal aesthetic is the game finding its own mythology. The player isn't building what Rome actually built. They're building what Rome might have become if it had a hundred years on a magic island and access to crystal energy.

This should feel EARNED, not arbitrary. The crystals don't appear from nowhere — by Level 15, the player has been diving for crystals, the narrative hints at the island's ancient power, the pyramid at the center has been vibrating faintly. The transformation at Level 18 is the island waking up to what the player has built.

---

### THE ATLANTIS RING SYSTEM

The defining visual structure of Era 3. Concentric water channels carved around the island center, connected by bridges, organizing the city into sacred zones.

**How it appears:** At Level 18, the terrain of the central island shifts. Canals begin to form — not excavated by the player, but revealed. The island's ancient structure was always ringed; the Era 2 city was built on top of it. As the crystal energy grows, the old ring channels fill with water. The city reorganizes itself around the rings.

**Ring 1 — The Sacred Ring (innermost)**
Radius: approximately 120px from center
Contains: The pyramid/temple, the high shrine, the crystal beacon (Level 22+), the Oracle's pool
Access: One bridge from the north, one from the south. The ring channel here is deep and still.
Character: Ceremonial. Near-silent except for the water. The crystal beacon at the pyramid apex casts shifting light down into the ring water. The water glows — a specific teal-blue-green that nothing else on the island matches.
Who walks here: The player, Livia (she becomes the Oracle figure in the narrative), the occasional pilgrim NPC in white. No market noise reaches here.

**Ring 2 — The Civic Ring (middle)**
Radius: approximately 260px from center
Contains: The forum, the grand library, the bathhouse complex, the arena
Access: Four bridges at compass points, wide enough for two people abreast
Character: The administrative and intellectual heart. Busy during the day. The forum fountain is now a crystal-fed cascade. The library gets a glass-crystal roof that refracts light inside.
Who walks here: Citizens, scholars, civic NPCs. Debate on the forum steps. Queue outside the bathhouse.

**Ring 3 — The City Ring (outer)**
Radius: approximately 400px from center (approaching island edge)
Contains: Residential insulae, the market district, the castrum, the trade harbor
Access: Six bridges, including the main dock road approach
Character: Dense, alive, loud. The market here is enormous. The castrum wall forms part of the outer ring's boundary.
Who walks here: Merchants, soldiers, civilians, arriving traders. The sound is fullest here.

**The Ring Water:** Not blue-grey sea water. A specific glowing teal — the color of crystal energy mixed with ocean. At day, it reads as unusual but natural. At night, it GLOWS. Soft pulsing luminescence, gentle waves catching and scattering the light. The rings visible from other islands at night look like halos around a city that shouldn't exist.

**The Bridges:** Era 3 bridges are stone-and-crystal. The arch is Roman, but the keystone and parapet caps are faceted crystal that catches light. At the center of each bridge, a small crystal lamp post that doesn't burn oil — it just glows.

---

### 1. Visual Transformation of the Island Surface

At Level 18, the island begins to crystallize from the center outward. It's subtle at first — the pyramid apex has a faint prismatic shimmer. The ring channels begin to emerge. The oldest stone buildings show crystal growth in their mortar joints, like the island is reclaiming them as part of itself.

By Level 21, the transformation is significant. The central Sacred Ring zone glows at all hours. Crystal spires have grown naturally from the H2 and H3 hilltops — they weren't placed, they emerged. The terrain reads as two eras overlaid: Roman stone infrastructure with crystal growth threading through it.

By Level 25, the island looks like an archaeological discovery from the future. The Roman city is still legible — the grid, the forum, the Via — but everything has been elevated. Hanging gardens cascade from the library walls. Crystal towers (not buildings — natural formations that have been shaped) mark the ring intersections. The pyramid at center is now a beacon visible from every other island.

**Color palette shift:** The warm stone and terracotta of Era 2 remain as the base. Layered over: teal and aqua crystal glow, gold prismatic light from crystal surfaces in sun, soft violet from the deep ring water at night. The island at noon looks like sunlight hitting a Roman city through stained glass. At night, it looks like a dream of a city, not an actual one.

---

### 2. Building Style Evolution

**Level 18–20:** The existing buildings transform rather than replace. Crystal veins run up column shafts. The temple pediment relief begins to glow faintly. New structures added at this tier are hybrid — Roman form with crystal elements. The bathhouse gains a crystal-heated pool that steams in cool air. The granary gets a crystal-powered irrigation system (no more hand-cranked well).

**Level 21–23:** Floating elements begin to appear. Not buildings — platforms. Crystal-anchored floating stone platforms hover above the ring channels, accessible by suspended rope bridges or crystal lifts. The library grows a floating observation deck. The arena gains a floating VIP box above the seating.

**Level 24–25:** Full Atlantis aesthetic. The pyramid becomes a pure beacon — the outer facing is now crystal, the interior inaccessible but glowing. Crystal towers 3x the height of any building frame the Sacred Ring. Hanging gardens on scaffold-less crystal armatures cascade from the civic ring walls. The lighthouse has been transformed: its rotating beam is now a crystal prism that throws colored light across the water at night — reds, greens, blues visible from the wreck beach.

**Materials:** Roman stone + crystal growth + living garden. The crystal isn't imported or manufactured — it grew here. The gardens aren't decorative — they produce. Everything in Era 3 feels like it has biological intelligence, as if the island is participating in its own construction.

---

### 3. Infrastructure

**Water (Era 3):** The aqueduct doesn't carry water anymore — it carries crystal energy. The channel still looks like water but it pulses with a faint blue glow. The actual water supply runs through an underground crystal-filtration system — street fountains now flow with fresh, cool water that has a faint teal tint. The ring channels themselves are the primary water infrastructure.

**Floating water:** At Level 22, a floating aqueduct appears — a suspended crystal channel that runs between H2 and H3 at height, crossing above the Via. Water flows along it, suspended. From below, you can see the water moving across the sky. It serves no practical purpose that the ground aqueduct didn't already serve. It exists to be impossible. The player built it because they could.

**Roads (Era 3):** The Via is now crystal-inlaid — the stone paving has crystal strips running along the curb lines that glow at night, providing gentle path lighting. The ring bridges replace the simple road network for inter-district travel. The dock road is the only Era 1 remnant — deliberately preserved, worn stone among the newer crystal-inlaid paving, showing where all of this began.

**Energy:** Crystal pylons at district corners channel solar energy (the day/night energy system now has a visual architecture to explain it). By Level 24, the whole island's energy is visibly networked — thin crystal fibers running between pylons, pulsing with stored sunlight.

---

### 4. Ambient Life

The island is a city now in every sense. Not just populated — teeming.

**Citizens:** Dozens of ambient NPCs across all three ring districts. The Sacred Ring has pilgrims and scholars. The Civic Ring has civic workers, guards, and students. The City Ring has merchants, soldiers, laborers, and travelers from the other islands (they come — the reputation of this place travels).

**Crystal fauna:** Bioluminescent jellyfish drift through the ring channels. Crystal-winged moths swarm the beacon at night. The cats that were in Era 1 are still here — they sleep on warm crystal surfaces.

**The original four NPCs:** They are civic leaders now. Livia is the Oracle, permanently near the pyramid. Marcus commands the castrum. Vesta runs the grand market. Felix is the harbor master and navigator. They are no longer the player's companions — they are the city's. The player built the city that gave them purpose.

**Floating life:** NPC-operated crystal skiffs drift through the ring channels. They carry goods, passengers, or just look decorative.

---

### 5. Lighting and Atmosphere

Era 3 has three distinct atmospheres that demand different emotional responses:

**Noon:** The crystal surfaces catch and scatter sunlight into prismatic colors across stone walls and canal water. The city dazzles. Shadows are not just dark — they have color in them, reflected from crystal surfaces nearby. The sacred ring shimmers. It's almost too beautiful to be practical.

**Sunset:** The crystal faces turn amber and red. The ring water catches the sunset and holds it. The beacon at the pyramid apex catches the last direct light of the day and throws a single golden beam east across the water — visible from the wreck beach. This is deliberate: the first time the player made fire on that beach, the night was dark. Now their fire is big enough to reach it.

**Night (the centerpiece):** The island glows. This is the visual climax of the entire game. The ring channels pulse teal. The crystal towers cast soft prismatic light onto the stone streets below. The lighthouse prism throws color across the water. The city ring is busy and bright. The sacred ring is quiet and luminous. The beacon at the pyramid apex pulses slow, ancient, steady — like a heartbeat.

From a ship at sea, from the wreck beach, from the other islands, the player's island is visible at night as a glowing shape on the horizon. Not just a light. A specific, complex, impossible glow. Their city. Their light.

---

### 6. Sound Evolution

**Era 3 soundscape:**
- The ocean is now beneath everything — not gone, but the city sounds sit on top of it
- Crystal harmonics: a constant sub-bass hum from the ring channels, just below perceptible — you feel it more than hear it. When the player stands near the ring water, the hum becomes audible as a chord.
- The beacon pulse has a sound: a deep, resonant tone on each pulse, like a massive singing bowl. It pulses every 8 seconds. You hear it from anywhere on the island.
- Ambient crowd: rich, layered, with specific sounds rising and falling — a laugh from the market, a chant from the sacred ring, the arena crowd building and releasing
- Crystal movement: when wind crosses the crystal towers and spires, they ring — a high, clear sound that travels across the island in gusts
- The floating aqueduct: the sound of water flowing overhead, which should be impossible, but is
- Lyre mode (Era 3): the lyre is joined by its echo. The same melody plays, slightly delayed, slightly different in pitch — as if the island itself is answering. Two lyres, one musician, one very old building that learned to play back.
- At night: the hum of the rings, the pulse of the beacon, and underneath it all, still, the waves. They're the last unchanged thing from Level 1.

---

### 7. The "Wow Moment"

**Level 22 — First night with the ring channels fully lit.**

The sun sets. The ring water begins to glow — first faintly, then with increasing confidence. The beacon at the pyramid top pulses on. The crystal towers on H2 and H3 catch the beacon light and refract it across the city.

The player is standing on the Sacred Ring bridge looking outward.

They can see the wreck beach in the distance — dark, as it always was. The beach where they had nothing. Where the fire was a prayer, not a declaration.

Their island glows between them and the horizon.

They press nothing. They just stand there.

That is the wow moment of Era 3. The game doesn't tell them they've won. The light does.

---

## THE FULL ARC: THREE FIRES

The campfire in Era 1 is the literal origin of the island. The player makes it. It's their first act.

The eternal civic fire in the forum (Era 2) is the campfire grown public. Not personal survival anymore — communal warmth. The fire that says "a city lives here."

The crystal beacon at the pyramid (Era 3) is the fire made myth. It doesn't burn. It doesn't consume. It radiates. It is the campfire that became permanent and forgot its origin and became something else — something that will outlast the player, the NPCs, and every storm the island will ever face.

The player lit the first fire. They built the last one.

That is the game.

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES FOR EACH ERA

### Era 1 additions needed (Levels 1–8)
- Campfire as interactable object with tending mechanic (not just decoration)
- Worn-earth path rendering that tracks player movement frequency
- NPC daily routine loops (morning/afternoon/evening positions)
- Chicken ambient AI with foraging, roosting, flock behavior
- First stone foundation for granary appears at Level 6 as terrain-layer
- The dock gains a ship carving relief at Level 7 (landmark)

### Era 2 additions needed (Levels 9–17)
- Via Principalis: straight paved road from dock to temple, visual layer
- Aqueduct: 5-arch structure from H2 to bathhouse (already specced in terrain doc)
- Ambient NPC system: 8–12 wandering citizens with simple patrol routes
- Castrum zone: barracks cluster + wall segment + gate at Level 13
- Lighthouse at east headland with rotating beam (already specced in terrain doc)
- Forum fountain: particle water effect at plaza center
- Street lamp posts: oil light pools along Via at night

### Era 3 additions needed (Levels 18–25)
- Ring channel terrain layer: three concentric teal-glow channels rendered under buildings
- Ring bridge sprites: 6 total, stone arch + crystal keystone
- Crystal vein overlay on existing buildings: starts sparse at 18, dense by 22
- Crystal towers on H2 and H3: emerge at Level 20
- Floating crystal platform: one above Sacred Ring at Level 21, purely visual
- Floating aqueduct: crystal channel suspended above Via at Level 22
- Beacon pulse system: slow pulsing glow from pyramid apex, visible at distance
- Night glow pass: all crystal elements emit additive teal light at night
- Era 3 lyre mode: echo layer on existing peaceful lyre (phase offset, slight detuning)
- Ring water sound: low crystal hum when player near channel
- Beacon sound: 8-second resonant pulse, audible island-wide

### Cross-era continuity rules
- The campfire must remain visible in Era 2 and Era 3 — it gets incorporated into the forum plaza as the eternal fire. Never remove it.
- The dock's rough wooden planks must remain visibly older than surrounding Era 2/3 stone — never upgrade the dock aesthetics fully. It's where everything began.
- The cats are always present. Every era. They sleep in the most beautiful spot available.
- The four NPCs' faces don't change. Their surroundings do. The player recognizes them.

## DISTANCE VISIBILITY — THE OTHER ISLANDS SEE YOU

At Level 20+, the home island should be visibly different when viewed from other islands. The current `drawArenaIsleDistant` / `drawConquestIsleDistant` rendering is silhouette-only. For Era 3:

- The beacon pulse should be visible as a slow light pulse on the home island silhouette when viewed from other islands
- The ring glow should make the base of the island silhouette slightly teal at night
- Trade ships approaching at night should use the beacon as a navigation point

This creates a feedback loop: the player looks back at home from a distant island and sees their own light. This is not a UI notification or a reward screen. It's a moment of pure recognition, the strongest kind of game feedback — the world reflecting what you've built.
